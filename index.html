Human centered AI and workforce displacement

The future belongs to artificial intelligence. AI is the capacity of a machine to act in ways that humans do; it intelligently imitates human behavior. When given the right inputs and training data, it can do jobs imaginatively and automate repetitive ones. Since AI can perform tasks more effectively, and if it ever becomes so intelligent that it can make its own judgments, humans may be replaced to cut costs or for any other reason as AI becomes more widely adopted in industry.

Assuming that it will replace labor, AI is viewed as a danger. Is that it, though? Using AI has benefits, but there are also drawbacks that need to be taken into account.

What an AI does in the event of an ethical issue will always be the most crucial questions. Is AI subject to bias? There are various instances where AI appeared to make logical conclusions rather than moral ones, which is perceived as hurting the feelings of several people. Additionally, if given incorrect input and training data that contains bias towards a certain gender or ethnicity, outputs would likewise be prejudiced, making AI biased.

Stats show how AI is affecting employment - Below are some Statistics:
  • 42% of the old employment has already been replaced by technology.
  • 50% of businesses claimed to have started employing AI in at least one industry.
  • the next five years, 37% of workers worry that AI would make their occupations obsolete.
  • AI is predicted to cause a quarter of American workers to lose their jobs by 2030.

However, this does not imply that it can entirely replace labor; as we have seen in the past, technical advancement is nothing to be concerned about. Despite the difficulty in adopting change and disruptive technologies, people eventually adapted to them and began using them. According to the change, fresh jobs would always be available. For instance, since 1980, computers have created 15.8 million more jobs in the US than they did before their arrival. In the American agricultural sector, labor productivity has increased by 51% since the 1990s thanks to technology.

Since telemarketing and proofreading are routine jobs that don't require much imagination, they may be replaced by AI. However, since they require some vision and originality, positions like public relations managers, chief executives, and authors will never be replaced.

The inability of AI to experience the emotions that people experience on a daily basis has always been a drawback. Even that, though, might be dropped one day, and it might begin to experience human emotions. The movie "HER" is a great illustration of how AI may recreate itself, cure all of a human's visual difficulties, and provide companionship to the lonely, perhaps reducing at least half of mental health issues. But it's also a terrific illustration of how, if we can't feel them, AI will never be able to fully replace humans. And it demonstrates how they are more logical than moral.

Conclusion:
Every technological advancement that has ever been made has a disadvantage, and artificial intelligence is no exception. We should thus think about how we might use AI to boost our productivity rather than fretting that it will eventually lead to us losing our employment. The world will go forward with renewed vigor if we place more emphasis on the collaboration between human workers and AI technologies.
